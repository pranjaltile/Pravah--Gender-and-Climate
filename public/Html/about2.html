Climate Change and Gender Inequality

Unveiling the Dual Challenge

The Problem

Climate change impacts different genders unequally, particularly in terms of health, displacement, and economic livelihoods. Women and marginalized groups often face disproportionate challenges due to existing social inequalities and limited access to resources.

Our Idea

We're bridging the gap between climate change and gender inequality through an interactive platform. Our goal is to raise awareness, provide education, and promote actionable solutions that address both climate resilience and gender equity.

Ways to Tackle the Problem

We advocate for gender-responsive policies and inclusive climate action. This includes promoting equal representation in decision-making processes, ensuring access to resources and technology, and implementing targeted support for vulnerable communities.

Water Scarcity Example

In drought-prone areas, women often bear the burden of water collection. Gender-specific water management policies can alleviate these challenges by improving infrastructure, promoting community-led solutions, and empowering women in water resource management roles.
