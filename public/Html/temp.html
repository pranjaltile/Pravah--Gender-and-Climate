Climate Change and Gender Inequality

Unveiling the Dual Challenge

The Problem

Climate change disproportionately affects different genders, particularly women, in terms of health, displacement, and economic livelihoods. Social inequalities exacerbate these effects, limiting access to resources and decision-making power.

Our Idea

We aim to bridge the gap between climate change and gender inequality through awareness and action. Our platform provides insights, tools, and resources to address this dual challenge effectively.

Ways to Tackle the Problem

We offer solutions like gender-responsive policies, improved access to resources, and promotion of inclusive decision-making for climate resilience. By empowering vulnerable groups, we can create more effective and equitable climate strategies.

Real-World Example: Water Scarcity

In regions affected by drought, women often face additional burdens as primary water collectors. Gender-sensitive water management policies can help alleviate these challenges and promote sustainable solutions that benefit entire communities.
